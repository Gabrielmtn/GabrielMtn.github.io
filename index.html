About

My adventure so far

  • 2010-2011

    My origin story

    I knew I wanted to change the world when I volunteered on a World Bank platform, supporting kids learning to be social entrepreneurs. It was an amazing experience!

  • 2011-2012

    Joining the startup community

    Between starting my first startup, winning a World Bank grant, moving to Boston, and volunteering all over Cambridge, this was a busy couple of years! Also, I'm getting my feet wet with code!

  • 2012-2013

    Headfirst into code

    This is the period I really dove into code. Between working on my second startup, using a LAMP stack, and going deeper on UI/UX and design, I grew into a frontend dev during this period!

  • 2013-2014

    Startup Institute and beyond!

    After chosing to shutter my second startup, I attended Startup Institute to to go deeper on my existing skills, and to diversify them as well. Upon leaving, I felt more confident that I'd be changing the world with my code.

Salvation Army - Vail Valley (in progress)

A client centric development experience.

The Vail Valley chapter of the Salvation Army has desperately needed an update to their website, along with the ability to continuously update their site. Understanding their requirements, I determined that teaching them the Wordpress platform would be an appropriate course of action.

I'm very proud to feature them as client, due to their mission to support individuals and families who are on hard times. Using their organizations brand guide, we're closing in on a final design for the site.

Artist's custom presentation

An exercise in content, and experience design.

Working with an artist whose work was primarily sold at events meant building a tool for him to display his custom work. Using his iPad webapp, the artist was able to engage and entertain passers-by through an iPad with the experience running near his work area. By leveraging his existing following, we ran analytics on his most popular pieces, and built them into an Impress.JS presentation.

Check out the presentation, or experiment with the Impress.JS library at the official website.

10 Apocalypse Predictions & Scares

A lighthearted timeline, and javascript experiment.

This lighthearted timeline was build to be an informative and interesting look at history, and a way to promote the Startup Institute Student Expo. The timeline is a one page webapp using Timeline.JS, an open source project by the knight lab. It’s a beautiful, responsive library that’s designed with an eye to both mobile and desktop experiences.
